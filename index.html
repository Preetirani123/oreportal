About Us

What We Do

OrePortal is focused on adding value to mining projects and operations through evaluation and implementation of sensor-based sorting systems. Our inhouse software, OreDicer, is used to evaluate the variation of grade (heterogeneity) within a nominated footprint to determine the value potential associated with bulk sorting.

Our range of services includes:

  • Deposit evaluations for bulk sorting potential using our in house software, OreDicer, with libraries for benchmarking against similar deposit types
  • Assessments of the potential to increase/reduce the size of a nominated economic footprint (eg. pitshell) due to implementation of sensor-based sorting
  • Bulk and particle sensor technology evaluations from bench scale test work to on-site pilot trials
  • Sorting model development with machine learning methods to identify applicable sensors and build predictive algorithms for elements, such as gold, that cannot be measured directly by sensors
  • Directing sensor-based sorting studies including road map development, coordinating vendor-based test work, monitoring of feed streams, sampling of stockpiles (stockpile sorting potential), interpreting results and guiding mining companies during the evaluation process
  • QA/QC of installed sensor-based sorting systems. Eg. directing sampling campaigns and analyzing sensor outputs

Meet The Team

Our Expert Consultant

Bern Klein

Ph.D., P.Eng.

Bern Klein

×
Bern Klein is a Professor of Mineral Process Engineering at the UBC Norman B. Keevil Institute of Mining Engineering. His PhD on the subject of “Dense Media Separation” lead to his research into pre-concentration and sensor based sorting with studies at INCOs Ni-Cu operations in Sudbury back in 1999. Since then has published more than 20 papers on the subject including the chapter on “Ore Sorting” which is presently being published in the SME’s Mineral Processing and Extractive Metallurgy Handbook. He is also co-founder of MineSense Technologies which is a technology company advancing sensor based systems for the mining industry. His research at UBC is focussed on advancing Sensor Based Sorting with a focus on developing approaches to assess the benefits of sensor based sorting for mining operations and prosects. He works with closely with industry to support studies aimed at assessing the potential for SBS for their operations and projects.
